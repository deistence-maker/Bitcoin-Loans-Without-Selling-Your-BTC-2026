## Introduction

One of the biggest challenges Bitcoin holders face is accessing liquidity without selling their assets. Selling Bitcoin may provide immediate cash, but it also removes your exposure to future price appreciation and may trigger taxable events.

Bitcoin-backed loans solve this problem.

They allow you to **unlock capital while keeping your BTC position intact**, making them one of the most powerful financial tools available to long-term holders.

---

## How Bitcoin Loans Work

At a basic level, a Bitcoin loan follows this structure:

1. You deposit Bitcoin as collateral  
2. A lender provides you with a loan (usually stablecoins or fiat)  
3. You maintain a required collateral ratio  
4. Once repaid, your Bitcoin is returned  

This system allows you to borrow without selling.

---

## Example Breakdown

Let’s say:

- You own 1 BTC worth $60,000  
- You borrow at 40% LTV  

You receive:

- $24,000 loan  

Your Bitcoin remains locked but still yours.

## Why Borrow Instead of Selling?

### 1. Maintain Long-Term Exposure
Bitcoin has historically appreciated over time. Selling removes your upside.

### 2. Avoid Taxes
Selling BTC may trigger capital gains tax. Loans typically do not.

### 3. Access Immediate Liquidity
You can fund:

- Investments  
- Business operations  
- Emergencies  

---

## Common Use Cases

- Real estate down payments  
- Trading opportunities  
- Short-term cash flow needs  
- Portfolio diversification  

---

## Risks You Must Understand

### Liquidation Risk
If BTC price drops significantly, your collateral may be sold.

### Platform Risk
Custodial platforms may introduce counterparty risk.

### Interest Costs
Loans must be repaid with interest.
